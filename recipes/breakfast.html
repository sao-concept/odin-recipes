Squash Blossom Crepes

Squash Blossom Crepes are a delightful and visually appealing dish that combines the delicate flavors of squash blossoms with a light and fluffy crepe batter.

The crepes are filled with a mixture of tender and slightly sweet squash blossoms, along with a choice of complementary ingredients like cheese, herbs, or vegetables.

Ingredients

For the crepes:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter

For the filling:

  • Fresh squash blossoms (around 10-12 blossoms)
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (such as mozzarella, feta, or goat cheese)
  • Fresh herbs (such as basil, parsley, or chives), chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the sauce (optional):

  • Tomato sauce or salsa
  • Sour cream or Greek yogurt

Steps

  1. Prepare the crepe batter:
    • In a mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour and salt.
    • In a separate bowl, beat the eggs, then add the milk and melted butter. Whisk until well combined.
    • Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients while whisking continuously until you have a smooth batter.
    • Set aside to rest for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Prepare the squash blossom filling:
    • Gently remove the stamen or pistil from each squash blossom.
    • Rinse the blossoms under cold water to remove any dirt or debris. Pat them dry with a paper towel.
    • In a separate bowl, mix together the grated cheese, chopped herbs, salt, and pepper.
    • Carefully stuff each squash blossom with a small amount of the cheese and herb mixture. Twist the petals gently to seal the filling inside.
  3. Cook the crepes:
    • Heat a non-stick skillet or crepe pan over medium heat. Lightly grease the pan with butter or cooking spray.
    • Pour a small ladleful of the crepe batter into the pan, swirling it around to spread it evenly.
    • Cook for about 1-2 minutes or until the edges start to lift and the bottom is lightly golden. Flip the crepe using a spatula and cook for another 1-2 minutes on the other side.
    • Transfer the cooked crepe to a plate and repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  4. Assemble the crepes:
    • Take a cooked crepe and place a stuffed squash blossom in the center. Fold the sides of the crepe over the blossom to enclose it.
    • Repeat with the remaining crepes and squash blossoms.
  5. Serve:
    • Place the filled crepes on a serving platter.
    • Optionally, you can serve the crepes with a side of tomato sauce or salsa and a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt.
    • Garnish with additional chopped herbs, if desired.
